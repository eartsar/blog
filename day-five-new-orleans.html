Day Five (New Orleans)

Posted on Mon 10 October 2016 in Eitan Posts

I will be returning, New Orleans...

French Quarter afternoon stroll

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 9, 2016 at 11:56am PDT

...Because there is still so much left to see and do. So many more music venues to check out. More events and festivals to attend. More people to see and meet. You are a city that only knows how to breathe and thrive and live, and you conveyed that to me all to well in only two days. I don't want to go to sleep, because it would mean that my stay here has concluded.

We slept in (only a little bit!) today, getting some much needed rest, and promptly made a beeline for Café du Monde - a huge outdoor cafe, and one of the most famous ones in the world. We waited in a line - one that could so easily be cut - and were rewarded with some really good live music right on the side of the street that started up as we arrived. Lucky!

live jazz while we wait for breakfast

A video posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 9, 2016 at 9:29am PDT

Upon getting to the front of the line, we realized just how packed this place is. It's organized chaos, with waiters expertly coordinating their various sections, rushing coffee and beignets to various customers. An older couple was ahead of us about to find a table, but since they seated four, I invited myself and Aron into their company. They were all too happy to oblige, and when we were finally seated, we got to talking about road trips. It was a good conversation, as they themselves were vacationing veterans, and shared with us some of their experiences. Our choice of food was all too simple. Café au Lait and beignets (sort of doughnut-ish). Iconic and necessary. The chicory coffee was so great, that I grabbed seconds - iced - to go. Other snacks of the day: pralines (a sort of pecan fudge-toffee thing), and snow cones.

café au lait with beignet

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 9, 2016 at 11:55am PDT

From the café, we meandered into the Frenchman Quarter Market, and perused the shops with all of their nicknacks on display. We spent quite some time just hanging around and listening to the music on the streets, and roaming the quarter. Aron informed me later that we had walked upwards of six miles. I believe it. Along the way, I fell victim to a street artist, dressed creepily in all while with white skin-paint and white lipstick, who tapped me on the shoulder. I jumped. Upon reading the sign by her feet, "If you holla, you owe me a dolla", I obliged and dropped a single into the bucket by her feet. She winked and blew me a kiss. That was a good start to the day. I wish I took a picture.

Later, just before sunset, we grabbed dinner at Royal House - a bowl of iconic gumbo. It was fantastic. I added in some Crystal Made-in-Louisiana hot sauce, because I ain't no wimp. It burned so good.

andouille and chicken gumbo

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 9, 2016 at 11:34pm PDT

I craved more music and booze, so we returned to Frenchman Street after sunset. Sunday proved to be a lot calmer, but there was still plenty of music to be had. We stopped at a few venues, including the one from the previous night, and were rewarded with some great blues artists and strong drinks. One drink, in particular - the Hurricane - is a sort of long island iced tea-esque concoction with juice and grenadine... a very dangerous drink. Only the thought of a severe hangover before an eight hour drive the next day got us back to the hotel at a "reasonable" hour.

waiting for an uber... with music

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 9, 2016 at 11:43pm PDT

It was fun, NOLA. Really fun. We leave for Dallas tomorrow. Check Instagram for a few more pictures.
