Day Seven (Santa Fe)

Posted on Tue 11 October 2016 in Eitan Posts

After hours of nothingness, we're surprised by a beautiful town in the mountains...

Approaching Santa Fe

A video posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 11, 2016 at 8:21pm PDT

...And are elated to roam the streets of Santa Fe, what may be the most adorable town in the entire country. It became immediately apparent that if New Orleans was a mecca for jazz, Santa Fe is a mecca for art. There are endless galleries, and there is no way we're going to be able to appreciate even five percent of what this awesome town has to offer. It was worth the drive.

This is saying something, because the drive itself was the most boring one yet. Going along the Texas/Oklahoma border yielded nothing of interest at all. Except one pit-stop that had an auto museum, that was kind of cool.

car museum somewhere in New Mexico

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 11, 2016 at 8:51pm PDT

We couldn't take any decent pictures - the town is really kind of short and narrow. The buildings are almost like... mud/clay buildings, except that's just an exterior shell. I'm not really sure what's up with the aesthetic, but it's hilariously cute, and super unique. It's really hard to get good angles of the streets to see anything of value, but there is value there in spades. We intend to go tomorrow morning, and will try our best to take pictures of some sites and sights.

Being in Santa Fe, it was only appropriate that we had some Spanish style food. We meandered into El Mason, and were rewarded with a fantastic meal.

Fried cheese, potatoes, chorizo, chicken, hot chocolate - Spanish style

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 11, 2016 at 8:40pm PDT

Our AirBnB room this time is with a young couple who are super chill and awesome. The house is beautiful, and we traded some VT goodies for some NM goodies. Two pepper beers? What's a pepper beer... We'll have to try it tomorrow when not on the road...

We'll spend some more time in Santa Fe, tomorrow. As per usual - fantastic town/city, and deserving of its own trip. We depart early tomorrow afternoon for Flagstaff, AZ. We won't be seeing the Grand Canyon tomorrow, though. Rather, we'll likely go the following day super early to see it at sunrise. Dope.
