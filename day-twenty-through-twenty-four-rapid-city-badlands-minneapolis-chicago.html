Day Twenty through Twenty Four (Rapid City, Badlands, Minneapolis, Chicago)

Posted on Fri 28 October 2016 in Eitan Posts

Is it sad that the majority of my US history comes from visiting memorials, and watching The West Wing...

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 24, 2016 at 8:58pm PDT

...rather than from the textbook readings of my education? It's not really surprising to me, especially when you consider the quality of displays such as the Lincoln Borglum Museum at Rushmore National Memorial. These exhibits do a good job of beckoning viewers to dig deeper and learn more about this country's history and its various endeavors. Also fitting that they'd place a national memorial in the middle of nowhere, South Dakota, where you'll have time to let it all sink in.

Seeing Rushmore in person was a bit as one would expect. The sculpture is massive (it is a mountain, after all), but the vantage point is far enough that the details of the Presidents' faces are softened by the distance to which they are viewed. Because of this, the display remains in the distance, and doesn't take up the visitor's entire view, which gives the impression that the work of art is smaller than it truly is.

Leaving Rushmore, we drove eastward, and before too long, arrived at another popular national park - the Badlands!

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 24, 2016 at 9:24pm PDT

Unlike Yellowstone, where you get some pretty vast variety of climates and creatures, the Badlands is an archiologist-or-geologist's paradise. It's a lot more easily driveable, with less wildlife, and the vistas are more of a wonder in the sense that it's a showcase to the power of a changing climate over time. It's inspiring much in the same way that the Grand Canyon is, and the sense of scale that each vista provides reminds the viewer how small one human really is. There are also rattlesnakes, and plenty of ledges to get nervous keeping balance on. Watching Aron skip along the edge of some of these vantage points really makes my gut sink, and I'm not sure if it helps bring out my inner courage or stupidity.

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 24, 2016 at 11:26pm PDT

Anyway, from there it was a long drive out to Minneapolis, where we had a nice room booked for the night. Minneapolis is one of the locations that I really had to cut from the trip to make room elsewhere (in this case, Cody Wyoming). It didn't take long to realize that we were really missing out on some fun stuff, though the city seems to have more to do when the weather is warmer. Still, we had some coffee, a light breakfast, and made sure to stop off at Electric Fetus before heading out. I picked up some stuff - apparently it was a sale day.

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 28, 2016 at 12:41am PDT

All of this was really just "stuff along the way" to Chicago, though, where we stayed with some friends for a few nights. They were also out in Vegas with us, though we admittedly didn't see too much of them there as we knew we were coming here later. The weather is wonderful and totally cooperated with us for the first half of the trip.

Chicago weather

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 28, 2016 at 12:42am PDT

Chicago feels really similar to New York in a lot of ways - something I'm sure many natives of both cities would get angry at me for saying. It has its food differences (Chicago dogs vs NYC dogs ... Chicago pizza vs NYC pizza) where both items are really entirely different beasts. They have their different sports teams (the amount of hype in this city over the Cubs is unreal right now), they have their different neighborhoods, their different accents, but ultimately, they felt very similar. Big buildings, a lot of people, true venues to see the country's "melting pot" premise in action.

We ended up doing a decent chunk of stuff while here, mostly things that I wanted to do for fun rather than to truly be inspired. Day one started with brunch at Little Goat diner. Aron was really excited for this, as the owner/head chef is a top chef winner. I'm pleased to say the food is really good - we had a lot of it, and it was all fantastic. Don't ask me to remember what I ordered, it was some Japanese egg thing with pork belly and it was perfect. I even got some granola to go for the road which I promptly ate the next day.

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 28, 2016 at 12:50am PDT

Bellies filled, we went to Shedd Aquarium. We didn't have a whole lot of time to spend here, but I've been to plenty of aquariums before. Shedd was really nice (it does usually get put on the top five lists for the country), in large part because of how modern and well kept it is (so much better than Boston), but it was really great seeing the beluga whales they have there. That made the visit worth it.

baby beluga in the deep blue sea...

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 28, 2016 at 12:45am PDT

After that, we were off to Second City - a famous comedy club that puts on a hell of a show (we saw 105th revue), and I'm also pleased to say that it was a smash hit for us. I had the pleasure of seeing Second City once in Toronto, which was easily the best comedy show I've ever seen in my life. This one approached that same level of hilarity (didn't quite hit it!), and was worth every penny and moment spent there. If you ever get the chance to see a Second City show - do yourself a favor and go. The third act for this one had one of the best comedy sketches I've ever seen in my life.

To finish off the evening, we went and got some authentic deep dish at Lou Malnati's. Before you groan at the choice, listen here. I've read more opinions on deep dish rankings than anything else on this entire trip, and if I've learned one thing, it's that five people manage to have six opinions on the best deep dish pizza in the city. It tasted really good, and while I prefer my thin crust pies, the gigantic mass that this city calls a pizza filled me with calories, bloat, satisfaction, and shame... all at once. It was great pizza.

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 28, 2016 at 12:44am PDT

Day two was a bit more low-key. The weather played nicely, so we decided to go back downtown. We perused the Field Museum, which had some really great exhibits, one in particular being Tattoo art and history. It was really interesting (par for the course response for museum reactions), especially so since it's a subject I knew nothing about at all. The rest of the day we dedicated to walking the city, and getting more food. What's for dinner? Japanese BBQ at one of the Gyu-Kaku locations, of course! It was really good. Afterwards we went home and had a game night with our friends. It was a really good time.

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 28, 2016 at 1:03am PDT

Tomorrow, we leave for Cedar Point. We'll be heading through some Amish country, I think, and I likely won't make another post until we get to Rochester (I won't be describing each roller coaster, to spare you, and you're welcome). We're on the last leg of the trip at this point, and while these last few trips will be a lot of fun for me, it won't be as interesting or novel to share. I'll likely end on a check-in from Rochester, and then maybe some sort of fancy reflection post when I get back.

Until then, there's always more on Instagram.
