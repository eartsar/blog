Day Six (Dallas)

Posted on Mon 10 October 2016 in Eitan Posts

My parents are going to freak out when they read this. Which is why I'm posting it a day late, after we've already left Dallas...

Texas Star

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 11, 2016 at 8:28pm PDT

...So they know that we're a-ok. We left New Orleans early and made a relatively uneventful trek across the bayou, sticking to the interstate. I read on a blog post somewhere that people from Louisiana are secretly afraid of accidentally driving into the bayou, so I just decided to not go near it, and admire the majestically ugly swampy areas... or what little I saw of them along the way. The state as a whole is flat, but hardly flattering. Gassing back up at a midpoint stop along the way, I noticed a police officer handling what appeared to be a very, very large gun.

More on guns later.

As we approached Dallas, we had a barbecue lunch at one of the endless barbecue smokehouses across the state. It was pretty good. We were too hungry, and forgot to take any pictures. Sorry about that.

At some point, through some idle google searching, we found out that the Dallas State Fair is going on this entire month! Eager to attend, we purchased two tickets and took the detour. Overall, Aron enjoyed himself, but it's not really my thing... the prices made the Champlain Valley Fair look like a steal, and the place was teeming with teenagers on dates, or parents with tots. We perused the grounds briefly before splitting.

Dallas arrival

A video posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 11, 2016 at 8:05pm PDT

We called into our AirBnB room, and informed the owners that we'd be arriving late due to the diversion. They were cool with it. Cool people in general - a couple and their mother. A very welcoming home, and the room is nice, albeit the whole house is adorned with more crosses than I knew could be acquired before being labeled a hoarder. We got to chatting, about our trip, a little bit about sailing (thanks, Gary!), comparing Texas to Vermont in terms of people and hobbies, and right as we were about to turn in, we touch on the subject of guns. Oh do these guys love their guns - they're collectors.

I would normally go into this in a lot of detail, but for many of you - this is no big deal. However, I promise you that my parents are already horrified. So that's why I'm going to post this picture, because in for a penny, in for a pound.
