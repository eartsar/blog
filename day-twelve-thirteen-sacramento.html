Day Twelve & Thirteen (Sacramento)

Posted on Mon 17 October 2016 in Eitan Posts

Vegas was fun, but our trip continues...

ayup

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 16, 2016 at 5:14pm PDT

...and while I'm not exactly sad to leave Las Vegas, I am sad to leave my friends. Our shared time is always way too short.

Vegas itself really pales in comparison to New Orleans, our other city in our trip that revolves around night life. Vegas is loud, it's artificial, it's commercial, and it's kind of insincere in how everyone's looking to have a good time. I can't help but continue to compare the two cities, because the parties in NOLA were much more soul fulfilling than anything else we've seen. While I suppose it's possible people actually enjoy blasting top 100 hits, and drinking copious amounts of booze, it's a lot more fun when the music is live and shifts with the energy of the crowd. The party there is a collaborative effort everyone contributes to, in order to have a good time. In Vegas, you either join in out of obligation, or get left behind. It really wouldn't be fun to come back here solo - I'm glad we met up with friends.

We woke up early on Sunday morning to check out of the hotel and head over to Lion Habitat Ranch, a lion sanctuary that has a collection of rescues, as well as others born on the sanctuary grounds itself. The sanctuary wasn't quite as big as I was expecting, but it was a good time. I got to make conversation with the sanctuary's volunteers and staff, asking them about the efforts they make in working there, and learned a good deal about the difficulties in maintaining the sanctuary, and some of the more academic and preservation projects that they host. I feel conflicted about the sanctuary - a lot of these animals couldn't exist in the wild, but it's still a bit sad to see the majestic creatures just getting by in relative captivity, even if the sanctuary seemed to me to be a more academic and humane effort. Needless to say, it was a more sincere attraction than the noise of the Vegas Strip.

At about half past noon, it was way beyond time for Aron and I to take off. We reluctantly said our goodbyes (we'll be seeing some of them in the near future in Chicago) and hit the road for Sacramento - a manageable nine hour hike north. Plus well over an hour of delays... we ended up arriving late at half past one in the morning. Thankfully, our Sacramento friends (who flew back from Vegas) were ready to receive us super late. After a quick game of Exploding Kittens, we crashed and slept in the following morning. Something we love about our Sacramento friends - they're huge foodies, and were eager to bring us to one of their favorite spots for a late brunch, The Waffle Experience, where we had a slew of delicious waffles (surprise).

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 17, 2016 at 9:19pm PDT

Bellies filled, we roamed a bit of the Sacramento Capital Mall. Regan - Ronald Regan everywhere. Also, the Governator. Aron and I were so exhausted, that we were cool taking it east in Sacramento for the day, rather than travel two hours into San Francisco (and back), and not recoup. Besides, I've been to SF a few times now, and it was nice seeing a slightly different side to California that isn't infected by the virus that is Silicon Valley.

Hours seemed to fly by pretty quickly, and before too long our foodie friends took us to have some seriously legit Mexican street food at El Paisano Taco Truck. Aron and I weren't sure how confident our stomachs were for it - PTSD from the Santa Fe breakfast burritos, probably - but after having a sampling of the, we quickly got a second helping. I had the carnitas tacos, and they were fantastic... and only a buck fifty a pop!

Best tacos

I'm going to end the post here, we're about to play some Settlers of Catan or something for a little game night before we leave tomorrow for Portland. Crater lake on the way... I'm worried about the weather. There's been some thunderstorms hitting the region as of late, but delaying a day in Sacramento doesn't solve the issue - the weather will be rainy (snowy by Crater Lake) regardless. It's going to be a pretty long trip (9 hours), and with less than ideal weather, probably making it take an extra hour or two longer if we play it safe. Hoping for the best, and that it clears out for the Portland to Yellowstone leg of the trip. Gulp.
