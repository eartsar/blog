Day Four (Gulf Shores, New Orleans)

Posted on Sun 09 October 2016 in Eitan Posts

We made our way westward, along the Gulf of Mexico...

Musical Expression

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 8, 2016 at 10:52pm PDT

...leaving Atlanta behind us after a very short stop. There was a tiny bit of hotel shenanigans from the night before, where the lady (older, very caucasian) at the desk insisted I had booked two nights, instead of one. I was also apparently named Ms. Angwe Something. I think she confused "Eitan" for "Anekwe" - the surname of someone else in the system, I presume. I told her as much. She insisted that she was correct, as is the right of one's elders, and that this was just one of the many oddities of "booking third party". She cancelled the second night, and we slept - no harm no foul. The next morning, I went to the front desk, where the lady (younger, African American) immediately looked at me funny and said "Angwe?". I could only reply, "Apparently". "But that's a Nigerian last name. You don't look Nigerian...". What else could I respond except simply, "I agree". We had a laugh. I hope no one was left without a room due these shenanigans beyond my control.

We decided to play it by ear, and allow ourselves to get distracted a bit today, after the very boring drive yesterday. We stopped at a place called First Watch, in Montgomery, Alabama. Apparently, this is a pretty well-known chain, but Aron and I have never been. The food was farm-fresh with "healthy options". I opted for a safer breakfast of eggs and its likely companions (dubbed the "Traditional Breakfast"), whereas Aron went after some breakfast tacos with Chorizo (I think), avocado, tomato, beans, and other goodies. Many of you know that I'm a picky eater, and I'm pleased to say that he had the tastier meal of the two of us (though mine was still really good!). Impressed with their granola, I grabbed a bag for the road. Their coffee was also really good. Overall, it was a really solid brunch, and I'd be likely to go back.

After leaving Montgomery - it's a bit like Albany, in that it's not all that interesting of a place for a capital - we headed southwest towards New Orleans, but decided to go an hour south out of the way to Gulf Shores Beach, Alabama and stick our toes in the sand. There were plenty of other people, but we found a spot to sit and relax. It was a welcome break from seeing roads for hours on end, and the time we allotted ourselves flew by in the blink of an eye. A bittersweet theme of the trip.

worth the detour

Finally, we arrived at New Orleans at sunset. Seeing swampland was a bit of a surprise, and we drove miles over water. We left the car with valet parking, and I obviously forgot my go pro in there, so I'll have to upload the few video clips I took later. After checking into our hotel (so fancy), we immediately went to Frenchman Street, where we filled our bellies with some pulled pork sandwiches. Stomachs filled, we roamed around, taking in our fill of artwork on display (for sale, of course), and live jazz music. Words don't really do it justice. It truly is a party, daily, in a city that never sleeps. Whereas Vegas has always felt like the party was a business, New Orleans feels like the party is a lifestyle.

A video posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 8, 2016 at 10:55pm PDT

We got back to the hotel at around Midnight (it's 1 A.M. personal time, and we woke up really early today...). We're completely spent. Aron and I are going to sleep in a bit tonight, and head to the French Quarter tomorrow during the day, when it's generally safer to roam around and explore. If we're feeling brave, we'll check out Bourbon Street (tons of debauchery, and sinful pleasures for all types) tomorrow. If we feel out of our league, I may drag us back to Frenchman Street for more live music. It really was fantastic, and New Orleans warrants an entire vacation of its own.

After today, I think it's finally sunk in that I'm on a road trip around the country. It didn't feel real until now. I've never been so far out of my comfort zone (who would honestly call me adventurous?) before. It's kind of nice.

I was a bit long-winded today - we did a lot! We've also passed the 1500 mile mark, so that's neat. As per usual, check out my instagram for more if you care to look.
