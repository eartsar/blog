Day Fourteen (Portland)

Posted on Wed 19 October 2016 in Eitan Posts

A beautiful drive north leads us to Portland, Oregon...

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 19, 2016 at 11:05am PDT

...where the mountains are of a completely different scale than those of the Green Mountains on the northeast, especially when you're winding around and through them the entire time. We were a bit worried about the drive being pretty rainy and treacherous, but luckily for us, our route mostly cleared up by Tuesday afternoon. We weren't completely lucky, though, as Crater Lake had absolutely zero visibility from the rim village, and was very icy. Because of this, we decided to skip, and get to Portland earlier. This was a huge disappointment for me - I had grown up with a massive atlus of the country that used to sit on top of our family's fridge for the longest time. I used to open it up, and look at various landmarks around the country, and one of the coolest ones that always stood out was the lake on top of the mountain in Oregon. Unfortunately, it'll be a sight for a different trip. We did get a bit of a consolation present in the form of clear skies above Mount Shasta, though (pictured above).

The drive, as a whole, was beautiful. Really breathtaking.

drive through California and Oregon

A video posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 19, 2016 at 11:30am PDT

We arrived in Portland at around 9:30, and called our good friend Janice, who grew up in the Mad River Valley like us. She told us to get our butts over to Fire on Mountain for a sampling of some of Portland's finest wings and sauces. Sounded good to us! We had penaut sesame, bourbon bbq, sweet bbq, and standard buffalo sauces. The wings were pretty good, and the cajun fries that we grabbed with them were fantastic with a banana barbeque sauce. Yum!

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 19, 2016 at 11:33am PDT

Afterwards we hit up one of the many bars on the southeast side - 2.50 pints, and quarter-per-play skee ball. Can't really beat that.

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 19, 2016 at 11:36am PDT

We've got a full day of Portland ahead, with zero plans... Here's to hoping for a break in the cloudy weather!
