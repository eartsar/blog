Day Twenty Five through Twenty Seven (Cedar Point, Rochester, Home)

Posted on Tue 01 November 2016 in Eitan Posts

Climactic thrills followed by familiarity...

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 30, 2016 at 9:07am PDT

...I can't really think of a more fitting closure for the trip. While we didn't get to head through Amish country on Friday (too far out of the way), we did take the time to visit some friends located in South Bend, Indiana along the way. We could definitely feel home drawing near. All mid-to-large cities from Indiana onwards to the east have a similar pseudo-rust-belty feel to them. The buildings are all outdated (they were modern once, I suppose), and not very flattering. Skylines are somewhat unimpressive, and the weather seems to be more cloudy than not, and yet somehow, people don't seem too miserable. Our minor detour aside, we arrived at our AirBnB location in Vermillion Ohio (near Sandusky) in a very low-key and uneventful few hours.

The following day brought more excitement. We met up with one of my best friends from college at the park right at opening time, and enjoyed a day of non-stop coasters for twelve fun-packed hours. For anyone who hasn't been to Cedar Point, they take their coasters very seriously, with some of the country's most intense finding an appreciative home in the park. We hit up most of the iconic ones, my favorites being Millenium Force and Valravn. I probably prefer Valravn the most, as it's likely the most intense coaster at the park while still being an insanely smooth and painless ride. Unfortunately, Top Thrill Dragster was closed due to winds, but we did all the other coasters worth doing. Thank goodness for the line-cutting pass!

Arrival

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 30, 2016 at 9:06am PDT

Forgive the lack of pictures for Cedar Point rides. It was a sea of humanity, and I had Aron hold my phone most of the time since he wears woman's jeans with vacuum-sealed tight pockets.

There was a lot more going on at the park beyond just coasters, though, as it was the last weekend of being open before closure. Cedar Point celebrates Halloween by opening up haunted rides and mazes throughout the month of October, and completely transforms after the sun sets. It's actually really stunning, and all of the park's staff is really energetic. They managed to spook me many, many times - I'm a really easy target.

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 30, 2016 at 9:10am PDT

We left the park by midnight, and after a few drinks (and gushing over our favorite rides), we went home. The following Sunday was an easy travel day, as Sandusky is a hop-and-skip west of Rochester, my college home turf. Sunday night game night, filled with fun and friends, was fantastic. I didn't get to see everyone (not by a long shot), so if you're reading this wondering why I didn't stop by... Rochester was just a coincidental drive-through - I'll see you soon!

ROC

The next morning, we got up early to hit some of my favorite spots. I have yet to see any coffee shop come even remotely close to my personal favorite, Joe Bean. We also hit up the Strong Museum of Play, which has an entire floor filled with retro arcade games - all of them in impeccable condition. Our brief Rochester stop ended with lunch at Dogtown, which is where you go for the most delicious heartburn. I skipped the garbage plate (if you know what that is) since I had to still drive six hours to get home. Call me risk-averse...

Just so happens my favorite food/drink spots lead to explosive combinations. I handled this with experience. Please, I'm a professional Rochesterian.

Those are only a few of my favorite spots in the small city, but they are probably the best ones. Rochester is a really odd place. It feels like it's way past it's prime, the days of Kodak and Xerox are pretty much over, but the city absolutely refuses to fall into a rut like those companies. It's a great place with thriving artists, plenty of academics, and some great "upstate New York" (read: bad for you) food. It never quite felt like home, but whenever I visit, it welcomes me back happily. And so I'll keep returning to visit. It was nice to share this piece of life with my brother, as Aron had never really been in Rochester before.

We left early this afternoon, and I write this post now from the living room of my parents' house. Needless to say, they were thrilled to see me again after four weeks. I also really welcome having a day to unwind, let it all sink in, and prepare for my return back to work on Wednesday.

home

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 31, 2016 at 10:21pm PDT

Tomorrow, day twenty eight, I'll really reflect on the trip as a whole and share my thoughts on what I liked the most, and what I would change if I were to do it all again sometime. It's still a bit surreal that I've been all over the country - from New Orleans to Portland - all within the past few weeks. So many places that I miss, and so many places that remind me of how much I love Burlington - a place that truly does feel more like home than any other.

I hope you'll come back to hear those thoughts. Thanks for letting me share these small pieces of my trip with you. The trip's over, but we'll always have Instagram.
