...Because we were so reluctant to leave **Santa Fe**. Honestly, what a great surprise this town has been - it's really an absolute gem in the middle of the desert and mountains.

We awoke early this morning to get a glimpse of the mountains at around sunrise. It was cold - maybe under forty five degrees - and that took me by surprise, since we had been traveling through 90+ degree weather to get there. We ended up befriending our host's *extremely friendly* dog, and it was really difficult to say goodbye this morning. After a brief farewell walk, and some belly scratches, we managed to say goodbye (to hosts, too - the goodbyes, not the belly scratches). Aron contemplated dog theft.

We ended up going to [The Pantry](http://www.pantrysantafe.com/), which is a local favorite with very typical New Mexico style food. We had two breakfast burritos - mine with chorizo, because chorizo is the best breakfast meat - and of course, chile on top... Christmas style. I liked the red chile's flavor a little better, but the green chile was also amazing (and really hot). It was easily the best breakfast burrito I've ever had. Spoilers: my stomach was not happy with me, and my reckoning came a few hours later with a hellfire inferno in my gut.

Afterwards, we decided to do a few more things before leaving for Flagstaff. While we didn't really have time to go back downtown (sorry, no pictures), we hit up this... art project thing, called [Meow Wolf](https://meowwolf.com/). It's like this interactive art-plus-story-plus-mystery-interactive-amusement-thing. It's hard to describe, but you try to derive the story via context clues... and they're everywhere. It was an insane experience, and is basically a point of pride and joy for residents of the city. I can see why. [Here's a writeup about it](http://arstechnica.com/gaming/2016/04/inside-meow-wolf-the-amusement-park-for-people-who-want-a-weirder-disneyland/). It's also the local maker space, which is really cool.

Alas, we could only stay in The City Different for so long, and by twoish, we were on our way to Flagstaff. 75 mph highways all the way, so I felt slightly less guilty going 90ish during the more secluded portions of the trip. The car handles really well, but it's small, and the crosswinds actually push us around quite a bit. It felt nice driving that fast without worrying about cops or other drivers, that's for sure. It was a quiet, but beautiful drive. Many trucks.

Our timing was perfect, and we arrived to the sight of a wonderful sunset.
