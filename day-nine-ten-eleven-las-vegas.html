Day Nine & Ten & Eleven (Las Vegas)

Posted on Sat 15 October 2016 in Eitan Posts

We woke up at 4 AM on Thursday morning...

Sunrise

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 13, 2016 at 12:21pm PDT

...to be able to view the Grand Canyon at daybreak. Everyone who has seen it before was telling me how necessary it was to see this this American wonder, but it was our hosts in Santa Fe that convinced us to delay our viewing to be at sunrise. They described the changing colors, the necessity of going a bit beyond just the safety of the railings, and the thrill of watching the sun rise over the eastern ridge. So we did so, and for the first time in the trip, Aron and I were cranky and not really willing to talk much.

But our grumpiness slowly faded as we approached the state park. We were one of the first ones to get to the observation point (Mather Point), and got prime seats. We couldn't really even see the canyon originally, because it was so dark. The sky out here is ridiculously crisp, and without any light pollution, we had a full view of the starry sky above. And so, we waited in below freezing weather, watching people slowly trickle in - dads with their sons, a few couples.

At about 5:30 in the morning, the sun's slight started to filter into the sky, and the early moments of sunrise began. With it, the canyon's color transformation started, going from a dusky grey, to a subdued blue, and warming to a soft orangey brown. It was truly incredible. So much so, that we decided to leave the safety of the railing, and go out onto one of the rocky outcrops jetting into the center of the canyon. It wasn't a particularly difficult climb down (a bit narrow, with a hell of a deadly drop if you stumble), but Aron and I played it a bit safe and went slowly. We were rewarded, as we so often are in this trip, with some killer pictures.

After we had our fill (we stayed a few hours), we got some quick breakfast, and crossed rocky desert roads to get to Las Vegas.

Vegas approach

A video posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 15, 2016 at 6:04pm PDT

I checked into our hotel rooms (three of them), and dumped off the car in the garage - we had friends to gather. It took a bit, but we eventually all got together and roamed the Vegas Strip. From there on, we had the usual fare of libations, liberations, and laughs, but we called it a bit early (we were tired). Friday is where the fun began.

A photo posted by Eitan Romanoff (@eitanromanoff) on Oct 15, 2016 at 9:50am PDT

Friday morning, we decided to get some of our "nerd fest" stuff out of the way. The "reason" for a lot of us coming out at once was due to a convention for some game I used to play, with some of my friends being those I met in the game, and others that I knew prior that joined along with me. So, since we dropped money on these tickets, we figured to at least show up at the keynote. In all honestly, it felt like a waste of time, so we all bounced right after and hit the strip again. We had a lot of good food, like crepes (we're at the Paris Las Vegas, after all!)...

... without any real plan or goal. Vegas is a good time with friends, though I have to say, compared to New Orleans, it's a bit underwhelming. Whereas New Orleans had the party as a lifestyle, it's just so commercialized here in Vegas. Ah well, it's nice to kick back, play, and drink.

There are so many other things I haven't shared here. Meals we had, and things we're doing. But Aron and I are late to go see Cirque du Soleil, and I have to end the post here! I'll have a part two after we arrive in Sacramento that goes over a bit more of the Vegas leg of the trip. In the interim, you can always check out Instagram for more pictures.
